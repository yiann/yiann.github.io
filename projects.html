UMooD: 2017 CHI Student Design Competition

UMooD is an ongoing project which is designed for people who suffer from depression. Some depression patients go seeing a therapist once a week or every two weeks. Our goal is trying to help them maintain a better life quality while not having treatments. Our team tries to empower them with an intervention which helps them get along with their mood and manage their daily life better.

Teachoard: Interaction Design Course Project

Teachoard is an ongoing course project which is designed for the elderly in Taiwan and their personal foreign caregivers. According to my interviews, the biggest problem for them is the language barrier. Therefore, Teachoard is a touch-screen board that can be attached to daily necessities. My goal is to let foreign caregivers learn the elderly's language in daily life. After the language barrier is broken down, they will be able to communicate with each other more and build a trustful relationship.

"Sun" Glasses: Critical Making Workshop Project

"Sun" Glasses is a pair of glasses that is designed to enable individuals to signal their openness to interaction, despite any facial expression that might betray their true intentions. Individuals will feel more comfortable in new cultural contexts, in that they can clearly indicate whether they do or do not want to socially interact. Our team built a hi-fi prototype with Arduino LilyPad and flex sensor.

Deloitte Design Jam

In the Deloitte Design Jam, our team designed a system for Zingerman's store manager to better forecast and manage customer demands on football game days. We took in factors, including weather or season effect, game schedule, day of the week or month and historical sale data, to provide managers suggestions on staff arrangement and materials ordering.

Jarvis: Designing For User Experience Course Project

Jarvis is an app for college students to better control their time spending on smartphones, and raised awareness of bad influences on using phones in the dark, finally decreased the total duration. Our team prototyped this design with Invision and got 80% positive feedbacks after user testings.
